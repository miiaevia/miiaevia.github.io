The Importance of Self-Care

When I was in High School, I was lucky enough to be taught something that many people don't learn until much, much later- the importance of self-care.

I was in a Peer Counseling program that involved listening to our fellow students who were struggling with something, and wanted to talk to a peer instead of an adult. The program was led by Mr. Cummings, who is the best teacher I've ever had. He'd been running the program for several years and he spent a semester teaching us things like listening skills and mandatory reporting before letting us loose on the school. Of these lessons, the one I've come to rely on over the years, and will continue to use for the rest of my life, was on the importance of taking care of ourselves.

In a human-centric field like counseling, it is very, very common for people to experience burn-out, from taking on too much and trying to help too many, without resting or taking care of themselves. However, this can be more widely applied to any stress-inducing situation. The crux of the lesson is this- only we are responsible for ourselves so only we can ensure that we take care of ourselves. No one else will do this for us.

The takeaway from this lesson is important. It means that we do not need to feel guilty whenever we need to take a step back from something or someone in order to rest and replenish our internal resources. Only we can meet this need and we have a responsibility to ourselves to do so. Stepping back temporarily from a challenging problem is also in our benefit, as unintuitive as that may seem. When problem-solving, it is common for people to work themselves into a loop, and repeat it fruitlessly, exhausting ourselves with no progress made towards a solution. Stepping back allows our sub-conscious to chew on the problem on its own, so that when we come back to it, there may well be a solution we hadn't thought of before, because we were stuck in our head.

To sum up, is it important to take care of ourselves, so that we can continue to press on long-term. Not only is it unnecessary to feel guilty for taking care of ourselves, but it also benefits those around us, as well as the problem we are trying to solve. So as we dive into new territory and take on bigger challenges, remember- you are responsible for taking care of you!
